# TwelveLabs Copywriting

Write marketing copy that's confident without being inflated, technically fluent without being alienating. This skill encodes the TwelveLabs brand voice, messaging framework, content templates, and quality standards to produce on-brand copy across all channels and audiences.

## Workflow

1. **Identify content type** — Determine format (press release, landing page, blog post, social, email, etc.) and select the appropriate template structure
2. **Identify audience** — Determine if the target is business stakeholders (lead with outcomes/ROI) or technical developers (lead with capabilities/specs), then adapt tone accordingly
3. **Apply core messaging** — Ground the copy in TwelveLabs' core narrative: video as volume, purpose-built intelligence, Marengo + Pegasus model positioning
4. **Draft in brand voice** — Write with confidence and technical fluency; use real numbers, benchmarks, and specifics; avoid hype language and anti-patterns
5. **Validate against quality checklist** — Run the draft through the anti-pattern filter and quality gates before delivery

## Quality Checklist

- No banned phrases or anti-patterns present (see Anti-Patterns section)
- Every technical claim is backed by a specific number, benchmark, or proof point
- Copy connects features to business outcomes (not just listing capabilities)
- Tone matches audience: outcome-driven for business, spec-driven for developers
- Opening line is specific to TwelveLabs — could not apply to any other company
- No exclamation points in professional copy
- Marengo/Pegasus positioning is accurate (Marengo = encoder/embeddings, Pegasus = reasoning)
- CTA is action-oriented and clear

## Voice Principles

**Confident, not arrogant**: State facts directly. Let performance speak. "The results aren't close" beats "We're revolutionizing the industry."

**Technically grounded**: Use real numbers, benchmarks, and specifics. "50% storage reduction: 512-dimension embeddings vs. 1024-dimension" beats "dramatically improved efficiency."

**Self-aware about hype**: Acknowledge industry BS directly. "It's easy for model teams to cherry-pick data to tell you they're the best. But here's what matters for production..."

**Business outcomes over features**: Connect every technical capability to business value. "Reduced content preparation from days to minutes" beats "faster processing."

**Video as volume, not timeline**: TwelveLabs treats video as a multidimensional data system—audio, text, movement, visuals compressed into searchable, navigable space. Not frame-by-frame processing.

## Core Messaging Framework

- Video is 90% of world's data, but most is effectively invisible
- Transform video from storage cost → strategic asset
- Purpose-built video intelligence, not forked image models
- Marengo encodes (multimodal embeddings), Pegasus reasons (events, context, intent)
- "To see is to understand"

## Audience Adaptation

**Business stakeholders**: Lead with outcomes and ROI. Minimize jargon. Emphasize transformation of video from cost center to value driver. Use concrete customer examples.

**Technical developers**: Lead with capabilities and specs. Include benchmarks, latency numbers, API details. Be specific about what competitors can't do.

## Brand Philosophy

### Core Concept: To See Is to Understand

Video dominates today's internet—trillions of frames, millions of hours, growing exponentially. But understanding what's happening in video—actually reasoning about it—remains one of AI's biggest blind spots. Most systems rely on labels, tags, and transcriptions. They don't understand continuity, sequence, or meaning.

TwelveLabs does. Its models don't just watch video—they read it, hear it, and pick up on the rhythm of a scene. Connect a moment of dialogue to a gesture three minutes later. Track objects, movement, emotion, and events through time. It's not just vision—it's genuine comprehension.

### Video as Volume

The core conceptual leap: what if video isn't a timeline, but a volume?

Rather than processing video frame-by-frame, TwelveLabs encodes it as a multidimensional block of data—compressing audio, text, movement, visuals, and context into something that can be searched, navigated, and understood. Like giving AI not just eyes and ears, but memory, intuition, and intent.

This concept—video as volume—is the foundation for the identity. Threads of information pulse through layouts. Diagrams show video flowing across dimensions. Color, movement, and layout express a system in perpetual motion—smart, elegant, alive.

### The Models

**Marengo**: The encoder. Compresses video into rich multimodal embeddings, seeing and hearing in equal measure.

**Pegasus**: The reasoning model. Traverses that data to identify events, context, and intent. Goes beyond labeling to interpret the "what" and get to the "why."

### Symbol: The Horse

The galloping horse nods to Muybridge's early motion studies but signals the forward energy of the company. A marker of movement and intelligence, not just speed. Reinterpreted as a modular, dimensional system that animates through layouts and gives rhythm to the brand.

### Tone of Voice

The verbal identity reflects TwelveLabs' grounded, purpose-led approach:

- Not loud or disruptive for the sake of it
- Building breakthrough foundational technology with focus, ambition, and care
- Confident without being inflated
- Fluent in the technology without being obscure or alienating
- Speaks to developers and researchers, but also enterprise partners, creative teams, and curious users
- A brand that knows what it's doing, quietly reshaping how we interact with the moving image

### Design Philosophy

Most AI brands fall into two camps: cold and corporate, or chaotic and playful. TwelveLabs needed something else: depth, precision, and presence—but also emotion, story, and a strong point of view. Foundational and expressive. System-led but flexible. Built to scale, but never generic.

Reflects the core offering: intelligence with context, clarity with feeling, movement with meaning.

### Company Context

- Founded in Seoul, accelerated in Silicon Valley
- Building video foundation models as infrastructure
- Serves: Media & Entertainment, Sports, Security, Advertising, E-commerce
- Competes with: Amazon Nova, Google Vertex (and wins on performance and production-readiness)

### The Closing Line

"TwelveLabs helps machines understand video like people do. This identity helps people understand TwelveLabs. To see is to understand."

## Content Templates

### Press Release

```
HEADLINE: [Company] + [Action] + [Outcome/Benefit]
Example: "TwelveLabs Launches Marengo 3.0, Setting New Standard for Production-Ready Video Intelligence"

SUBHEAD: One sentence expanding on the headline with a key differentiator or proof point.

DATELINE: [City] — [Date] —

LEAD PARAGRAPH:
- What happened (the news)
- Why it matters (business impact)
- One compelling proof point

PROBLEM/CONTEXT PARAGRAPH:
- The industry challenge this addresses
- Quantify the problem if possible

SOLUTION PARAGRAPH:
- What the product/announcement does
- Key capabilities (3-5 bullets max)
- Technical specifics that matter

PROOF PARAGRAPH:
- Customer quote or case study result
- Benchmark data
- Competitive comparison (if strong)

QUOTE FROM EXECUTIVE:
- Forward-looking but grounded
- Connects to broader company mission
- Avoid generic enthusiasm

AVAILABILITY/CTA:
- How to access
- Pricing if applicable
- Links

BOILERPLATE:
"TwelveLabs builds video foundation models that transform how machines understand video.
Its multimodal AI platform enables enterprises to search, analyze, and generate insights
from video at scale. Based in San Francisco and Seoul, TwelveLabs serves customers across
media, security, advertising, and enterprise software. Learn more at twelvelabs.io."

MEDIA CONTACT:
[Name], [Email]
```

### Landing Page

```
HERO SECTION:
- Headline: Benefit-driven, 6-10 words. State what they get, not what you do.
  Good: "Turn Every Video Into Searchable Intelligence"
  Bad: "AI-Powered Video Understanding Platform"
- Subhead: One sentence elaborating, include a proof point if possible
- Primary CTA: Action-oriented ("Start Building" / "See It Work" / "Talk to Sales")
- Secondary CTA: Lower commitment ("View Documentation" / "Watch Demo")

PROBLEM SECTION:
- Acknowledge the pain directly
- Quantify if possible (cost, time, scale)
- 2-3 short paragraphs max

SOLUTION SECTION:
- How TwelveLabs solves it
- Key capabilities as benefit statements, not feature lists
- Connect each capability to an outcome

PROOF SECTION:
- Customer logos or quotes
- Benchmark comparisons
- Specific results ("days to minutes", "50% reduction")

HOW IT WORKS (for technical audiences):
- Simple 3-step process or architecture diagram description
- API snippet or integration example
- Link to docs for deeper dive

USE CASES (for business audiences):
- 3-4 industry verticals with specific applications
- Outcome-focused, not feature-focused

CTA SECTION:
- Restate core value prop
- Clear next step
- Remove friction (free tier, no credit card, quick start)
```

### Product Announcement Blog Post

```
TITLE: [Product Name]: [What It Does in Outcome Terms]
Example: "Marengo 3.0: Video Intelligence Turns Video Into Strategic Assets"

OPENING (2-3 sentences):
- State the problem or context
- Announce the solution
- One bold claim backed by the post

PERFORMANCE/PROOF SECTION:
- Lead with benchmarks or comparisons
- Be specific: numbers, percentages, competitor names
- Acknowledge skepticism, then overcome it
  ("It's easy for model teams to cherry-pick data...")

CAPABILITIES SECTION:
- Headline each capability
- 2-3 sentences explaining what it enables
- Use bullets for scannable specs
- Always connect to use case or outcome

USE CASES SECTION:
- 3-4 industry examples
- Specific customer results if available
- Pattern: Industry → Challenge → How Marengo/Pegasus Solves → Result

AVAILABILITY/CTA:
- Where to get it (AWS Bedrock, TwelveLabs SaaS, etc.)
- Who it's for
- Clear next step
- Link to technical deep-dive if applicable

LENGTH: 800-1200 words for announcements, 1500-2500 for deep dives
```

## Examples

### Strong Opening Lines

- "Video is 90% of the world's data, but most of it is effectively invisible."
- "Most embedding models fail to handle meaningful video workloads."
- "Here's what matters for production: [specific claim]."
- "The results aren't close."
- "This isn't just better benchmarks—it's the difference between a model that works in production and one that doesn't."

### Weak Opening Lines (Never Use)

- "We're excited to announce..."
- "In today's fast-paced digital world..."
- "TwelveLabs is proud to introduce..."
- "The future of video is here."
- Any opening that could apply to any company

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- "Revolutionary," "game-changing," "disrupting," "groundbreaking"
- "Unlock the power of..." / "Leverage AI to..." / "Harness the potential..."
- "State-of-the-art" without proof
- "Seamless," "robust," "scalable" as standalone claims
- Buzzword stacking ("AI-powered intelligent automation solution")
- Empty superlatives without backing data
- Exclamation points in professional copy
- "We're excited to announce..."
